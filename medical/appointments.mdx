## In-person appointments

In-person consultations at our Indore office provide comprehensive medical care with hands-on physical examination and immediate access to diagnostic services.

### What to bring to your appointment

Ensure you have everything needed for a productive visit:

- **Valid identification** - Government-issued ID or Aadhaar card
- **Insurance information** - Insurance card or policy details if applicable
- **Medical records** - Previous test results, imaging reports, or records from other healthcare providers
- **Medication list** - Current medications with dosages, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements
- **Symptom diary** - Notes about your symptoms, when they started, and what makes them better or worse

<Note>
  Arrive 10-15 minutes before your scheduled appointment time to complete any necessary paperwork and check-in procedures.
</Note>

### Preparing for your in-person visit

<Accordion title="First-time patients">
  New patients should arrive 20 minutes early to complete registration forms and provide medical history. Download and complete new patient forms from [indoredoctor.com](https://indoredoctor.com/) in advance to save time.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Annual physical examinations">
  For comprehensive physical exams, you may need to fast for 8-12 hours if blood work is required. Confirm fasting requirements when scheduling your appointment.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Follow-up appointments">
  Bring records of symptoms, blood sugar readings, blood pressure logs, or other monitoring data requested during your previous visit.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Chronic disease management visits">
  Have your medication list updated and note any side effects or concerns you've experienced since your last appointment.
</Accordion>

## Telemedicine appointments

Telemedicine provides convenient access to Dr. Kothari's expertise through secure video consultations. This option is ideal for follow-ups, medication management, and many common medical concerns that don't require physical examination.

### When to choose telemedicine

Telemedicine appointments are appropriate for:

- **Medication refills** - Routine prescription renewals for ongoing treatments
- **Follow-up consultations** - Checking progress after starting a new treatment
- **Chronic disease monitoring** - Regular check-ins for diabetes, hypertension, or other chronic conditions
- **Lab result discussions** - Reviewing test results and discussing next steps
- **Minor acute illnesses** - Cold, flu, allergies, and other non-emergency conditions
- **Skin concerns** - Visual evaluation of rashes, lesions, or skin changes
- **Health counseling** - Nutrition advice, lifestyle modifications, and wellness guidance
- **Mental health support** - Anxiety, stress management, and emotional wellness consultations

<Info>
  Telemedicine may not be suitable for conditions requiring physical examination, in-office procedures, or diagnostic tests. Dr. Kothari will let you know if an in-person visit is necessary.
</Info>

### Technical requirements

Ensure you have the necessary technology for a successful telemedicine appointment:

- **Device** - Smartphone, tablet, laptop, or desktop computer with camera and microphone
- **Internet connection** - Stable broadband or mobile data connection (minimum 2 Mbps recommended)
- **Video platform** - Access to the telemedicine platform used by our practice (instructions provided upon booking)
- **Quiet, private space** - A location where you can discuss your health concerns confidentially
- **Good lighting** - Adequate lighting so Dr. Kothari can see you clearly during the consultation

### Preparing for your telemedicine appointment

<Steps>
  <Step title="Test your technology">
    Check your device, camera, microphone, and internet connection 15 minutes before your appointment. Join the video call a few minutes early to resolve any technical issues.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Gather necessary information">
    Have your medication list, symptom notes, and any relevant health records or test results ready to share during the consultation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Prepare your environment">
    Find a quiet, well-lit, private space where you won't be interrupted during your appointment.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Have measurements ready">
    If you're monitoring specific health metrics (blood pressure, blood sugar, weight, temperature), take these measurements before your appointment.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Join the consultation">
    Log in to the telemedicine platform at your scheduled appointment time and wait for Dr. Kothari to join the video call.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### During your telemedicine visit

Your virtual consultation will be similar to an in-person visit:

1. **Discussion of concerns** - Describe your symptoms, health concerns, or reason for the appointment
2. **Medical history review** - Dr. Kothari will ask about your medical history, current medications, and lifestyle factors
3. **Visual assessment** - If needed, Dr. Kothari may ask you to show specific areas of concern or demonstrate movements
4. **Diagnosis and treatment plan** - Receive a diagnosis (if applicable) and personalized treatment recommendations
5. **Prescriptions** - Prescriptions can be sent electronically to your preferred pharmacy
6. **Follow-up instructions** - Get clear instructions for follow-up care, monitoring, and when to schedule your next appointment

<Note>
  Telemedicine appointments are conducted through secure, HIPAA-compliant video platforms to protect your privacy and medical information.
</Note>

## Appointment types and duration

Different appointment types require different amounts of time. Understanding this helps you schedule appropriately and plan your day.

| Appointment type | Typical duration | Best for |
|-----------------|------------------|----------|
| New patient consultation | 45-60 minutes | First visit, comprehensive evaluation |
| Annual physical examination | 45-60 minutes | Complete health assessment, preventive care |
| Follow-up visit | 20-30 minutes | Monitoring chronic conditions, treatment progress |
| Acute illness consultation | 20-30 minutes | Cold, flu, minor injuries, new symptoms |
| Telemedicine appointment | 15-30 minutes | Medication refills, test result review, minor concerns |
| Emergency consultation | 30-45 minutes | Urgent medical issues requiring prompt attention |

## Cancellations and rescheduling

We understand that schedules change. If you need to cancel or reschedule your appointment:

- **Advance notice** - Provide at least 24 hours notice when possible
- **Contact methods** - Call our office or use the online portal to cancel or reschedule
- **Rescheduling** - We'll help you find another convenient appointment time
- **Late cancellations** - Repeated late cancellations may result in scheduling restrictions

<Info>
  Canceling with advance notice allows us to offer your appointment slot to another patient who needs care.
</Info>

## Frequently asked questions

<Accordion title="How far in advance should I book?">
  For routine appointments, booking 1-2 weeks in advance is recommended. For annual physical examinations, schedule 2-4 weeks ahead. Urgent consultations can often be accommodated same-day or next-day.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do you offer weekend or evening appointments?">
  Appointment availability varies. Contact our office to inquire about weekend and evening slots to accommodate your work schedule.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I request a specific appointment time?">
  Yes, we'll do our best to accommodate your preferred time. Flexibility increases your chances of getting your desired slot.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if I'm running late?">
  Call our office immediately if you're running late. Depending on the delay and schedule, we may be able to see you or may need to reschedule.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can family members attend my appointment?">
  Yes, you're welcome to bring a family member or caregiver to your appointment for support, translation, or assistance.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are telemedicine appointments as effective as in-person visits?">
  For many conditions, telemedicine is just as effective as in-person care. Dr. Kothari will let you know if an in-person visit is necessary for proper evaluation or treatment.
</Accordion>
